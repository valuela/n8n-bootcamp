# The Complete n8n Bootcamp

## Professional Automation Engineer Skill Tree

**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Skill Tree provides students with a visual and structured roadmap of the professional skills developed throughout the bootcamp.

Unlike the curriculum, which is organized by modules, the Skill Tree is organized by competencies.

Students progress by mastering skills rather than simply completing lessons.

Every completed project, lab, assessment, and capstone should unlock new competencies within this tree.

The objective is to become a well-rounded Automation Engineer rather than someone who only knows individual n8n nodes.

# Skill Progression Levels

Every skill progresses through four levels.

## Level 1 — Awareness

The student understands the concept.

---

## Level 2 — Application

The student applies the skill with guidance.

---

## Level 3 — Professional

The student independently applies the skill to realistic business scenarios.

---

## Level 4 — Mastery

The student can teach, improve, optimize, troubleshoot, and defend the skill during interviews.

---

# Unlock System

Students unlock skills by completing:

- Lessons
- Labs
- Projects
- Design Challenges
- Debugging Exercises
- Portfolio Improvements
- Mock Interviews

Watching videos alone does not unlock a skill.

Practical demonstration is required.

# Completion Criteria

A branch is considered complete when the student can:

- Explain the concepts.
- Apply the skills independently.
- Build a professional project.
- Debug failures.
- Document the implementation.
- Present the solution.
- Answer interview questions confidently.

The goal of this Skill Tree is not to collect completed lessons.

The goal is to develop professional competency through deliberate practice.

Students who complete the entire Skill Tree should possess the technical depth, engineering mindset, business understanding, and professional communication skills expected of a job-ready Automation Engineer.
